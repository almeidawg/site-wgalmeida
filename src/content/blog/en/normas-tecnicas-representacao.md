# Technical standards in project representation: NBR 6492 and NBR 10068

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Engineering & Execution*

---

## Introduction

Premium projects require clear technical language. The NBR 6492 and NBR 10068 standards standardize graphic representation and sheet formats, ensuring precise understanding among client, team, and approval bodies.

---

## NBR 6492: representation of architectural projects

NBR 6492 defines **graphic representation conditions** so the project is well understood. Among the main items are definitions of:

- **Site plan** and **location plan**
- **Building plan** (horizontal section at 1.50 m)
- **Sections** and **facades**
- **Details and enlargements** at appropriate scale
- **Scales** and reading criteria
- **Program of needs** and memorials

The goal is to avoid ambiguities and ensure the drawing communicates what will be executed.

---

## NBR 10068: drawing sheets, layout, and dimensions

NBR 10068 standardizes **sheet formats** and technical layout organization. It defines:

- A series (A0 to A4) and their dimensions
- Title block positioning
- Margins and frame
- Center and cut marks
- Reference grid system

This standardization facilitates reproduction, filing, and reading by any professional.

---

## Why this matters in practice

- **Better communication** between architecture, engineering, and construction
- **More clarity** for approvals in official bodies
- **Less rework** during execution
- **More predictability** in the schedule

---

## Conclusion

Technical standards are not bureaucracy. They are the foundation of operational excellence. A well-represented project reduces risks and elevates delivery quality.
