# Moodboard: The Visual Map of Your Project

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Interior Design*

---

## Introduction

A moodboard is the instrument that turns words into image. It aligns expectations, defines the project's atmosphere, and guides the curation of materials and finishes.

---

## What a good moodboard solves

- Eliminates ambiguities between client and team
- Avoids rework in material choices
- Ensures visual coherence across all spaces

---

## Essential elements

- Main and secondary color palette
- Materials and textures (floors, finishes, fabrics)
- Furniture and millwork references
- Lighting and atmosphere
- Decorative elements with identity

---

## How we build it at WG Almeida

1. Briefing and lifestyle analysis  
2. Curation of reliable references  
3. Selection of real materials and textures  
4. Panel assembly and client presentation  

---

## Digital, physical, or per space

- **Digital** for initial approval
- **Physical** for final material decisions
- **Per space** for large projects

---

## Conclusion

The moodboard is where the concept takes shape. When it is well built, the project becomes clearer, more elegant, and coherent.

## Example Process

### Project: Jardins Apartment - 180m2

**Briefing:**
Couple, 40 years old, no children, likes to host friends, prefers sophisticated yet welcoming spaces.

**Defined Concept:**
Contemporary with classic touches, palette in earthy tones with gold as the highlight metal.

**Moodboard:**
- Colors: Beige, caramel, terracotta, white
- Materials: Travertine marble, freijo wood, linen, velvet
- Metal: Brushed gold
- Style: Straight lines with occasional curves

**Result:**
Elegant and timeless environment that reflects the residents' personality and meets their functional needs.
