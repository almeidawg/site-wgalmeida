# Style guide for residential spaces: find your identity

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Interior Design*

---

## Introduction

Choosing a style is not just an aesthetic matter. It is a way to translate **personality, routine, and sensoriality** into architecture. In premium projects, style guides every decision.

---

## 1. Minimalism

Clean lines, few elements, and focus on the essential. Minimalism favors functionality and visual calm.

**Ideal for:** those seeking organization, serenity, and timeless spaces.

---

## 2. Classic

Balanced proportions, noble materials, and discreet elegance. The classic style values impeccable finishing and refined details.

**Ideal for:** those who appreciate tradition, symmetry, and lasting sophistication.

---

## 3. Modern

Striking volumes, contemporary materials, and integrated technology. Modern balances visual impact and practicality.

**Ideal for:** those who like current design, strong lines, and expressive spaces.

---

## 4. Industrial

Exposed structure, concrete, metal, and wood. Industrial reveals technique and turns raw material into language.

**Ideal for:** those seeking urban personality with authenticity.

---

## 5. Vintage

A mix of references, affective value, and objects with history. Vintage values memory and detail.

**Ideal for:** those who like welcoming spaces with a unique identity.

---

## 6. Tropical

Natural materials, balanced colors, and connection with the outdoors. Tropical favors light, texture, and lightness.

**Ideal for:** those who want well-being and a sunny atmosphere.

---

## How to define the right style

- Analyze your routine and real needs
- Separate reliable visual references
- Combine style with functionality
- Avoid trends with no coherence with the project
