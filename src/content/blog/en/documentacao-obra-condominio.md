# Condo renovation documentation: notice, ART/RRT, and responsibility

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Engineering & Execution*

---

## Introduction

Renovations in condominiums require specific documentation to ensure safety, control, and compliance with NBR 16280. Organizing these documents avoids delays and ensures predictability.

---

## Work or renovation notice

The notice records essential data:

- Types of intervention (demolition, electrical, plumbing, gas)
- Technical responsible professional (engineer or architect)
- Project delivery and ART/RRT
- Planned start and end dates

This document formalizes the scope with the administration.

---

## Responsibility term

The term states that the owner assumes responsibility for the work and compliance with:

- NBR 16280
- Owner's manual
- Current legislation
- Internal bylaws and regulations

---

## Documents normally required

- Renovation plan signed by a qualified professional
- Construction schedule
- Project and drawings with ART/RRT
- Technical statement about structural impact
- List of authorized professionals

---

## Conclusion

Correct documentation reduces legal and operational risks. In premium works, excellence starts at the documentation stage.
