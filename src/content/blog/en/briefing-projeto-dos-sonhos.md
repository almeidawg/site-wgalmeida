# Briefing: How to Plan the Project of Your Dreams

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Architecture & Design*

---

## Introduction

The briefing is the step that defines the success of the project. It is when we translate lifestyle, priorities, and expectations into clear guidelines for precise and personalized architecture.

---

## Why the briefing is decisive

- Avoids rework and late changes
- Directs investment to what really matters
- Ensures coherence between concept and execution

---

## The dimensions that guide the project

1. **Resident profile** and family dynamics
2. **Routine and lifestyle** (work, leisure, hosting)
3. **Aesthetic preferences** and visual references
4. **Functional needs** and storage
5. **Technology and automation** desired
6. **Budget and priorities** that are realistic
7. **Schedule and deadlines** expected

---

## How to prepare

- Separate reliable references (Pinterest, magazines, real images)
- Align expectations with everyone who will live in the space
- Define investment limits and priorities

---

## What happens next

1. Technical analysis of the information
2. Moodboard and aesthetic direction
3. Layout study
4. Concept presentation

---

## Conclusion

A well-led briefing is the first sign of a premium project. It brings clarity, avoids noise, and protects the client's investment.

### 2. Copying Third-Party Projects
What works for others may not work for you. Seek inspiration, not copying.

### 3. Ignoring the Future
Design thinking about the next 5-10 years, not only the current moment.

### 4. Not Involving All Residents
Unilateral decisions generate conflicts during the project.

### 5. Focusing Only on Aesthetics
Functionality is as important as beauty.

---

## Briefing Checklist

Use this list to prepare:

**About the Residents**
- [ ] List of all residents
- [ ] Special needs
- [ ] Future plans

**About Style**
- [ ] Reference photos (minimum 20)
- [ ] Favorite colors
- [ ] Materials you like

**About Function**
- [ ] List of required spaces
- [ ] Storage needs
- [ ] Special equipment

**About Investment**
- [ ] Budget defined
- [ ] Clear priorities
- [ ] Desired deadline
