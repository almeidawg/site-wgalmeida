# Phases and timelines of the architectural project: from survey to executive

*By Grupo WG Almeida | Architecture & Design*

---

## Introduction

A premium project demands method, clarity, and sequence. Each phase has a specific technical function and ensures the final result is buildable, elegant, and consistent.

---

## What is included in a complete architectural project

A high-end architectural project includes, among other items:

- Site plan, layout, and roof plan
- Demolition plan and construction plan
- Electrical and plumbing installation plans
- Finish plans and floor layout
- Ceiling and lighting plan
- Millwork layout and elevations
- Construction details (stairs, guardrails, roofs)
- Stonework and frames detailing
- Glass detailing (shower enclosures and mirrors)

---

## Complementary projects

Architecture integrates with a series of complementary projects, such as:

- Lighting design
- Hydrosanitary
- Structural and foundations
- Automation and rainwater reuse
- HVAC, exhaust, and gas

---

## Reference phases and timelines

Timelines vary by complexity, approvals, and interactions with third parties. Below is an overview:

| Phase | Reference timeline |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Survey (measurement) | up to 7 business days after signing |
| 2. Briefing | up to 5 business days after survey |
| 3. Site plan | up to 5 business days after briefing |
| 4. Preliminary study | up to 10 business days after site plan |
| 5. Complementary projects | depend on third parties |
| 6. Coordination | up to 20 business days after complementary |
| 7. EVF (financial feasibility) | up to 10 business days after phase 6 |
| 8. Legal project | 15 calendar days after phase 6 |
| 9. Preliminary design | 30 business days after phase 4 |
| 10. Executive project | 30 business days after phase 6 |

---

## Critical point: client validation

Timelines depend directly on client validations. In general:

- One revision is included in the preliminary design.
- Additional considerations may impact the schedule.
- After executive delivery, the project moves to construction.

---

## Conclusion

Well-defined timelines ensure predictability and an elegant process. When each phase is respected, the project gains consistency, reduces rework, and protects the investment.
